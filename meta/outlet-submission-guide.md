# Op-Ed Submission Guide

**Version**: 1.0
**Last Updated**: November 12, 2025

A comprehensive guide to submitting op-eds to target outlets, organized by tier according to The Statecraft Blueprint's wedge strategy.

---

## Strategy Overview

**The Wedge Approach**: Don't go straight to Tier 1. Build credibility through Tier 3, use clips to pitch Tier 2, then leverage established credibility for Tier 1.

**Timeline**:
- **Months 1-3**: Tier 3 outlets (easier entry)
- **Months 6-9**: Tier 2 outlets (with Tier 3 clips)
- **Months 12-18**: Tier 1 outlets (with established credibility)

---

## Tier 3: Entry-Level Placements

**Goal**: Build clips, establish credibility, reach insider audiences
**Priority**: Start here first

### The Hill

**Focus**: Congressional news and insider politics
**Audience**: Congressional staffers, lobbyists, policy professionals
**Why Target**: Staffers read it religiously; influences actual legislation

**Submission Guidelines**:
- **Word Count**: 600-800 words
- **Format**: Email submission
- **Contact**: opinion@thehill.com
- **Pitch**: 2-3 sentence summary + why timely
- **Response Time**: 1-2 weeks
- **Bio**: Brief (25-50 words)

**What They Want**:
- Timely to current Congressional action
- Insider perspective or unique angle
- Concrete policy recommendations
- Bipartisan appeal preferred

**Fit for Statecraft**:
- ✅ "Congress's Public Voting System" (structural reform)
- ✅ "Why Shutdowns Keep Happening" (timely, Congressional focus)

**Focus**: Cross-promotion with aligned writers
**Audience**: Their existing audience

**Approach**:
1. Identify Substacks in adjacent spaces (governance, policy, systems thinking)
2. Subscribe and engage genuinely
3. Pitch guest post that adds value to their audience
4. Offer reciprocal guest post opportunity

- Tech governance (Platformer, if appropriate)

## Submission Best Practices

### Before Submitting

**Research the Outlet**:
- Read 5-10 recent op-eds
- Understand editorial voice
- Check if topic has been covered recently
- Note word count and style

**Tailor Your Piece**:
- Match word count exactly
- Adjust tone to outlet
- Use examples that resonate with audience
- Frame argument for their readership

**Prepare Your Pitch**:
- 2-3 sentence summary
- Why now (timeliness)
- Why you (credentials)
- 50-word bio
- Include relevant clips

### Submission Email Template

```
Subject: Op-Ed Pitch: [Compelling Title]

Dear [Editor Name if available, or Editors],

I'm submitting an op-ed titled "[Title]" ([word count] words) for your consideration.

[2-3 sentence pitch explaining the argument and why it's timely]

I write about governance architecture at The Statecraft Blueprint (statecraftblueprint.org). My work has appeared in [Tier 3 clips]. [One sentence of relevant expertise or unique perspective].

The piece is below. Thank you for considering.

Best regards,
Jason Edwards

Bio: Jason Edwards writes about governance architecture and systemic reform at The Statecraft Blueprint. His work focuses on institutional design, democratic innovation, and engineering approaches to policy challenges.

---

[FULL OP-ED TEXT]
```

### Follow-Up Protocol

- **If no response in 2 weeks**: One polite follow-up email
- **If no response after follow-up**: Move on
- **If rejected**: Ask for feedback, revise, submit elsewhere
- **If accepted**: Respond quickly, be flexible with edits

### Simultaneous Submissions

**Generally NOT acceptable** for traditional outlets (WaPo, NYT, Bloomberg, Atlantic, etc.)

**Sometimes acceptable** for:
- Different tiers (submit to The Hill while also pitching Medium)
- Very different audiences (tech publication + policy publication)
- After reasonable wait time with no response

**Always acceptable** for:
- Your own Substack (publish there after waiting for outlets)
- Social media adaptations (T3/T4/T5 content)

## Outlet-Specific Variants

Some pieces may need significant adaptation for different outlets:

### Example: "Congress's Public Voting System"

**For The Hill** (750 words):
- Focus on immediate Congressional implications
- Cite specific recent votes
- Emphasize bipartisan support for reform

**For Washington Post** (800 words):
- Current version works well
- Broader audience framing
- Clear connection to shutdown or current crisis

**For Bloomberg** (800 words):
- Emphasize economic costs of gridlock
- Cite lobbying expenditure data
- Frame as market efficiency issue

**For The Atlantic** (1,200+ words):
- Add historical depth (secret ballot movement)
- Include more narrative elements
- Connect to democratic decline themes
