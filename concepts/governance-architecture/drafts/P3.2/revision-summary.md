## Recommendations for Glossary Additions

### Game Theory Terms to Add:

1. **Nash Equilibrium**
   - Definition: A situation where no player can benefit by changing their strategy while other players keep theirs unchanged
   - Example in content: Media race to the bottom in prisoner's dilemma
   - Relevance: Explains why rational actors produce suboptimal collective outcomes

2. **Prisoner's Dilemma**
   - Definition: A scenario where individual rational choices lead to collectively irrational outcomes
   - Example in content: Media outlets competing on sensationalism
   - Relevance: Core to understanding why "everyone should just do better" doesn't work

3. **Collective Action Problem**
   - Definition: Situations where individuals would benefit from cooperation, but each has an incentive to free-ride
   - Example in content: Voter education, rational ignorance
   - Relevance: Explains why individual solutions fail for systemic problems

4. **Race to the Bottom**
   - Definition: Competition that drives quality or standards progressively lower
   - Example in content: Media competition for clicks, political competition for soundbites
   - Relevance: Describes the dynamic outcome of the incentive structures

### Why These Terms Matter:

These game theory concepts are fundamental to understanding:
- Why good intentions aren't enough
- Why institutional solutions fail
- Why the system produces bad outcomes even with good actors
- Why changing incentive structures is necessary

They'll recur across multiple pieces in The Statecraft Blueprint and are central to systems thinking about governance.
